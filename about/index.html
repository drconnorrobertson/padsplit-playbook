About the Book

The most practical guide ever written for operating PadSplit at scale.

PadSplit Playbook: Scaling Affordable Housing Through Shared Living is what it sounds like — a working operator's manual for the most under-discussed corner of residential real estate.

The shared-housing model is not new. Rooming houses, boarding houses, and SROs have housed working Americans for a century. What's new is the operating layer: technology, marketing, tenant matching, and weekly payment infrastructure that turns shared housing from a logistical headache into a scalable real estate strategy.

PadSplit is the largest of those operating layers. This book is the deep dive into how to use it well.

Why this book exists

Most real estate books fall into one of two categories: motivational ("you can do this!") or theoretical ("here is a cap-rate formula"). Neither helps you on Friday afternoon when a member calls about a broken washing machine, or when you're trying to decide whether the four-bedroom with one bathroom is worth converting.

This book is the missing layer: the operational details, the underwriting templates, the conversion checklists, the conflict-resolution scripts, and the scaling decisions that separate operators who run one property from operators who run a hundred.

What's inside

The book is organized in ten chapters, moving from the macro (why shared housing is the right answer to a market problem) to the micro (how to pick the right kind of doorknob lock).

Chapters cover:

  • The PadSplit business model from the operator's perspective
  • Property selection criteria that actually predict performance
  • The full conversion process with budgets, timelines, and material lists
  • Underwriting frameworks with stabilized PadSplit revenue assumptions
  • Member relations, occupancy management, and conflict resolution
  • Maintenance, turnovers, and the operations cadence of a healthy property
  • Scaling — from one property to a portfolio
  • The economics and ethics of operating affordable shared housing
  • Marketing, branding, and the long-term moat of being a quality operator
  • Exit strategy, refinance, and the financial trajectory of a PadSplit portfolio

What this book is not

This book is not a sales pitch for PadSplit, and it is not affiliated with the company. It is an independent operator's perspective on using the platform — which means it will tell you what works, what doesn't, and where the rough edges are.

Who should read it

If you own one rental and want to understand whether converting it is worth the trouble, this book is for you. If you own twenty and you're deciding whether to add a PadSplit vertical to your operation, it's for you. If you're brand new to real estate and you're trying to figure out where to start, it's for you — shared housing has the highest ratio of revenue-per-dollar-of-acquisition-cost in residential real estate, and it's where smart small operators are starting.

It is also written for the policy people — the housing nonprofits, the city planners, and the public officials who need a real-world understanding of how shared housing actually operates if they're going to make decisions about it.

The bigger picture

America is short several million units of affordable housing. Building new supply at the workforce price point is structurally hard — land, labor, and materials make new construction unaffordable for the renters who need it most. Shared housing is one of the few levers that adds usable bedrooms to the affordable supply without requiring new construction.

That's the throughline of the book: shared housing is a real business and a real solution. You can build a profitable portfolio and help solve a problem at the same time. The two are not in tension. The operators who understand that are the ones who are going to win this decade.
