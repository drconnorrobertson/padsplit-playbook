About the Book

A field manual for the shared-housing operator

PadSplit is one of the most disruptive real estate models of the last decade — a workforce housing platform that rents rooms (not units) on a weekly basis. This book is the playbook the platform doesn't ship: the math, the conversions, the operations, and the lessons learned at the property level.

1

Built from Operator Experience

Written from the perspective of property owners who have actually converted, leased, and operated PadSplit properties — not theory.

2

Math That Actually Works

Underwriting frameworks, room-by-room rent stacking, and stabilized cash-flow models you can paste into your own deals.

3

Mission Plus Margin

How shared housing fits into the affordable housing solution — and why mission-driven operators are winning this market.

Who It's For

If you own — or want to own — rental property

This book is written for the working operator. If you fall into any of these camps, the playbook is for you.

Buy-and-Hold Investors

You own single-family rentals and you're looking for a way to increase yield without buying more property. Co-living conversion can lift gross rents 1.5–2x.

House-Hackers

You're living in your first property and want to maximize the spare rooms. PadSplit can pay your mortgage with margin to spare.

Affordable Housing Builders

You believe rental housing should be accessible. Shared housing is the most under-utilized lever in the affordability toolkit.

Aspiring Operators

You've never owned property but want to start. The shared-housing model has the highest revenue-per-dollar-invested in residential real estate.

What you'll learn inside

  • The PadSplit business model — how it works, what it charges, who lives there
  • Property selection criteria: which homes convert well, which ones don't
  • Room counts, square footage thresholds, and shared bathroom ratios
  • The full conversion process — bedrooms, locks, furniture, utilities, internet
  • Underwriting with stabilized PadSplit revenue — and the margin of safety to use
  • Member relations, conflict resolution, and how to keep occupancy above 95%
  • Scaling from one property to a portfolio of 10, 25, or 100 doors
  • The economics of affordable shared housing as a social good
